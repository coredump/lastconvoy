# Story — Last Convoy

## Premise

Humanity has lost its home.

A techno-organic infection has spread across the planet, corrupting machines, infrastructure, satellites, and orbital debris. The organism does not simply destroy technology — it **assimilates it**, merging biological growth with mechanical structures and using those hybrid forms as predators.

A last civilian evacuation convoy has launched into space, attempting to reach a distant **warp gate** before the infection can overtake them.

You are not a pilot.

You are not a soldier.

You are **a maintenance worker** — a custodian assigned to clean the lower decks of one of the convoy ships. You were on shift when the evacuation order came. There was no time to disembark.

The ship left with you on it.

Now you are the only person close enough to the rear turret to operate it.

Your job is simple:
**hold the line while the convoy escapes.**

---

## The Situation

The convoy is fleeing **away from the infected world**, but the further it travels, the more dangerous the enemy becomes.

The infection has already spread beyond the planet:

- Infected satellites
- Corrupted ship hulls
- Weaponized debris
- Assimilated orbital machinery

These fragments drift through space until the infection **rebuilds them into hunting forms**.

What pursues the convoy are not fleets or species, but **machines rewritten by a parasitic intelligence**.

---

## Your Role

You have no combat training.

You found the turret station unlocked, its operator gone or dead. The controls were simple enough to figure out. You had no choice.

You cannot fly the vessel.
You cannot turn around.

You can only move vertically along your mount and fire into the pursuing swarm.

Enemies close in from behind.
If they reach the defensive barrier protecting the ship's core systems, they begin **breaching the hull**.

The shields were already damaged when you sat down.

Every breach weakens the convoy's chances of survival.

---

## Combat Dynamics

The pursuing machines form a relentless wall of pressure.

Some are small scavenger drones.
Others are massive constructs assembled from warped mechanical frames and infected growth.

The infection favors **symmetry broken by organic corruption**: mechanical bodies partially consumed by alien tissue that glows with hostile energy.

The longer the convoy travels, the more sophisticated these constructs become.

---

## Escalation

As the convoy leaves the planet behind, the enemies do not disappear.

Instead, they grow stronger.

The infection has learned to survive in space, feeding on radiation, wreckage, and abandoned technology drifting through orbit.

Each region of space reflects a new stage of the infection's evolution.

---

## Biome Progression

### 1. Infected Atmosphere
The convoy launches through a chaotic sky filled with corrupted drones and broken aircraft pulled into the infection.

### 2. Low Orbit
Weaponized satellites, defense platforms, and orbital wreckage drift through the debris field, now animated by the infection.

### 3. Outer System
The infection stabilizes and spreads through deep-space debris and abandoned infrastructure.

Enemies become larger and more organized.

### 4. Deep Space Corridor
Far from the planet, the infection has fully adapted to space.

Its creations are massive hybrid constructs assembled from ancient wreckage and alien growth.

---

## End Goal

At the end of the journey lies the **warp gate**, the last hope for escape.

Your task is to keep the pursuing infection from breaking through long enough for the convoy to reach it.

If the defense fails, the infection catches the convoy.

If you succeed, the fleet jumps to safety — leaving the infected system behind.

---

## Characters

Characters appear at key story beats: **run start, biome transitions, and run end** (success or failure). Each beat is a short exchange — a few lines at most. No cutscenes, no long monologues.

The custodian does not have a portrait. The player is them.



### You — The Custodian
Middle-aged. Assigned to clean the lower decks. Nobody looks twice at the custodian.

You used to be a pilot. A good one. Life fell apart — the details don't matter anymore — and this was the work you could get.

When you sat down at the turret, the controls felt familiar in a way you weren't expecting.

You haven't told anyone.

### Rael — Ship's Engineer
Late twenties. Frighteningly smart. Before the evacuation she was rewriting drive firmware for fun.

Now she's the one routing power to your weapons systems, fabricating upgrade components from salvage, and transmitting them to your station between waves. She talks fast, jokes too much, and never lets you see that her hands are shaking.

She is terrified. She will not say so.

### The Entity
No name. No intent in the human sense.

It does not hate the convoy. It does not pursue out of malice or hunger. It expands because expansion is what it does — the same way a tide rises, the same way a fire spreads into dry wood.

It has absorbed millions of ships, thousands of habitats, an entire planet's worth of machinery. Each new structure becomes part of the whole without ceremony. The infection does not conquer. It continues.

The constructs it sends after the convoy are not soldiers following orders. They are extensions of a single process that has not noticed — and would not care — that some things are still trying to leave.

It is not evil.

It is indifferent in a way that is worse.

**Presentation:** The Entity is never shown. No portrait, no avatar, no face. It is heard only — transmissions bleeding through the ship's comms, fragmented signal patterns, corrupted audio. It does not speak to the crew. It speaks through the noise.

### Voss — Acting Commander
Was supposed to be third in command. Here to observe, to learn, to stay out of the way for a few years until they had enough experience to matter.

The captain is dead. The first officer is dead. Voss is seventeen and now running the bridge.

They give orders clearly. They make decisions quickly. They do not flinch where the crew can see them.

They have no idea if any of it is right.

---

## Tone

The story is not about defeating the infection.

It is about **surviving long enough to escape it**.

You are not saving the world.

You are a cleaner who sat down at the wrong console at the right moment.

You are guarding the last ships that remain.
